<p><p>Food ultrasonic testing systems are categorized into two main types: low intensity and high intensity. Low intensity ultrasonic testing is primarily used for non-invasive quality control, such as detecting surface and structural flaws in packaging and food products, ensuring safety and integrity. In contrast, high intensity ultrasonic testing focuses on food processing applications, such as emulsification, homogenization, and enhancing extraction. This type enhances product quality and consistency by breaking down particles and improving texture, flavor, and shelf life.</p></p>
